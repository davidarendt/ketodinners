Spinach & Feta Frittata

⏱ 20 min 🍽 4 servings ✓ Strict Keto
310Calories
22gProtein
23gFat
4gNet Carbs

A restaurant-quality frittata started on the stovetop and finished under the broiler — creamy egg custard packed with wilted spinach, salty feta, and sweet red onion.

Ingredients
Frittata
·8 large eggs
·3 oz crumbled feta cheese
·3 cups fresh baby spinach
·1/2 small red onion, thinly sliced
·2 cloves garlic, minced
·3 tbsp heavy cream
·2 tbsp olive oil
·1/2 tsp dried oregano
·Salt and black pepper to taste
Simple Green Salad
·4 cups mixed greens
·2 tbsp olive oil
·1 tbsp red wine vinegar
·Salt and pepper
Instructions
1
Preheat Broiler
Set oven broiler to high. Whisk together eggs, heavy cream, oregano, salt, and pepper until smooth and slightly frothy.
2
Sauté Aromatics
Heat olive oil in a 10-inch oven-safe skillet (cast iron is ideal) over medium. Add red onion and cook 3–4 minutes until softened. Add garlic and cook 30 seconds.
3
Wilt Spinach
Add spinach to the pan and toss until wilted, about 2 minutes. Spread evenly across the bottom of the pan.
4
Add Eggs
Pour egg mixture evenly over the vegetables. Scatter feta across the top. Cook undisturbed over medium-low for 4–5 minutes until edges are just set but center is still jiggly.
5
Broil to Finish
Transfer pan to broiler 4–6 inches from heat. Broil 2–3 minutes until top is golden and center is fully set. Watch closely — it can go from perfect to overdone quickly.
6
Rest and Slice
Let frittata rest 2 minutes before slicing into wedges. Serve with simple dressed greens alongside.

Notes

A 10-inch cast iron pan is ideal — it retains heat evenly and goes straight from stovetop to broiler.
Don't overcook on the stovetop before broiling — a jiggly center is correct at that stage.
Frittata is excellent cold or at room temperature — great for next-day lunch.
Add leftover roasted vegetables or cooked bacon to the egg base for easy variations.
