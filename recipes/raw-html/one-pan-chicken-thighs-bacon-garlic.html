One-Pan Chicken Thighs with Bacon & Roasted Garlic

⏱ 30 min🍽 4 servings✓ Strict Keto
480Calories
42gProtein
32gFat
4gNet Carbs

Chicken thighs and bacon rendered together in a single cast iron, whole garlic cloves roasted until jammy and sweet alongside, everything finished with fresh thyme and a splash of chicken broth to deglaze all the beautiful fond. Hearty, simple, and deeply good.

Ingredients
·4 bone-in skin-on chicken thighs
·6 strips thick-cut bacon, cut into lardons
·1 head garlic, cloves separated and peeled
·4 sprigs fresh thyme
·1/4 cup chicken broth
·1 tbsp olive oil
·Salt and black pepper
Instructions
1
Render the Bacon
Heat oil in a large cast iron over medium. Add bacon and cook until crispy and fat rendered, 5–6 minutes. Remove bacon; leave all the fat in the pan.
2
Sear the Chicken
Raise heat to medium-high. Add chicken skin-side down. Sear 6–7 minutes without touching until skin is deeply golden and crispy.
3
Add Garlic
Flip the chicken. Add whole peeled garlic cloves around the chicken. Cook 3–4 minutes until garlic starts to soften and turn golden.
4
Finish in the Oven
Add thyme sprigs. Transfer the whole pan to a 400°F oven for 15 minutes until chicken reaches 165°F and garlic is completely soft and jammy.
5
Deglaze & Serve
Remove from oven. Remove chicken and garlic to a plate. Add broth to the pan and scrape up all the bacon-chicken fond over medium heat. Return everything to the pan and scatter crispy bacon over top.

Notes

The whole peeled garlic cloves roasted in bacon fat become sweet and spreadable — they're almost a condiment. Don't skip them.
This is an extremely high-fat, high-flavor dish — the bacon fat, chicken skin fat, and garlic all combine into something extraordinary.
If the garlic cloves are large, make a small slit in each one so they cook through in time.
