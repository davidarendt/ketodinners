Eggs en Cocotte with Cream & Herbs

⏱ 20 min 🍽 4 servings ✓ Strict Keto
310Calories
16gProtein
27gFat
3gNet Carbs

Individual eggs baked in ramekins with a pool of heavy cream, fresh thyme, chives, and parmesan until the whites are just set and the yolks run like velvet. The most elegant 20-minute dinner.

Ingredients
Eggs en Cocotte
·8 large eggs
·1/2 cup heavy cream
·1/4 cup grated parmesan
·2 tbsp fresh chives, minced
·1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves
·2 tbsp butter for greasing
·Flaky sea salt
·White pepper or black pepper
·Pinch of nutmeg
Simple Green Salad
·4 cups mixed greens
·2 tbsp olive oil
·1 tbsp white wine vinegar
·Pinch of salt
Instructions
1
Preheat & Prep
Preheat oven to 375°F. Butter 4 individual ramekins generously. Set a deep baking dish or roasting pan to use as a water bath.
2
Set Up Cream Base
Spoon 1–2 tbsp of heavy cream into the bottom of each ramekin. Add a pinch of thyme and a twist of white pepper.
3
Add Eggs
Crack 2 eggs carefully into each ramekin, keeping the yolks intact. Spoon another tbsp of cream over each. Season with a pinch of flaky salt and nutmeg.
4
Top with Cheese & Herbs
Scatter parmesan over each ramekin, then chives and remaining thyme.
5
Water Bath & Bake
Place ramekins in the deep baking dish. Pour enough hot water into the outer pan to come halfway up the sides of the ramekins. Bake 12–15 minutes until whites are set but yolks are still runny.
6
Serve Immediately
Remove carefully from water bath. Serve in the ramekins immediately with simple dressed greens alongside.

Notes

The water bath (bain-marie) is essential — it ensures gentle, even heat that sets the whites without rubber-izing the yolks.
Timing is critical and varies by oven and ramekin size. Start checking at 12 minutes.
A thin layer of cream on the bottom prevents the eggs from sticking and adds richness.
This is a very low-carb, high-fat meal — perfect keto macros from something that looks incredibly fancy.
