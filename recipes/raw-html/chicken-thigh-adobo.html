Chicken Thigh Adobo

⏱ 30 min🍽 4 servings✓ Strict Keto
410Calories
38gProtein
26gFat
4gNet Carbs

The national dish of the Philippines — chicken thighs braised in a punchy, savory-sour mix of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and black pepper until the sauce reduces to a sticky, lacquered glaze. Deeply satisfying and unlike anything else in the rotation.

Ingredients
·4 bone-in skin-on chicken thighs
·1/3 cup soy sauce (or coconut aminos)
·1/3 cup white cane vinegar or white wine vinegar
·1/2 cup water or chicken broth
·6 cloves garlic, smashed
·3 bay leaves
·1 tsp whole black peppercorns
·1 tbsp avocado oil
·1 tsp monk fruit sweetener (optional)
Instructions
1
Marinate (Optional)
Combine soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns. Add chicken and marinate 30 minutes or overnight. The longer the better.
2
Sear the Chicken
Remove chicken from marinade (reserve it). Heat oil over medium-high. Sear thighs skin-side down 4–5 minutes until golden and the fat renders. Flip and sear 2 minutes.
3
Braise
Add reserved marinade and water/broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer. Cover and cook 20 minutes until chicken is cooked through.
4
Reduce the Sauce
Remove chicken. Raise heat to medium-high and reduce the braising liquid 5–7 minutes until it becomes a thick, glossy sauce. Taste — it should be savory, tangy, and slightly sharp.
5
Glaze & Serve
Return chicken to the pan and turn to coat in the glaze. Serve over cauliflower rice with extra sauce spooned over.

Notes

Vinegar is the key ingredient — don't substitute with lemon. The acid is what makes adobo taste like adobo.
This actually improves dramatically the next day as the flavors deepen. Make extra.
Coconut aminos makes a slightly sweeter, lower-sodium version that still works well.
