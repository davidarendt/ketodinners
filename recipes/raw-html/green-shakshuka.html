Green Shakshuka

⏱ 25 min🍽 4 servings✓ Strict Keto
300Calories
16gProtein
22gFat
7gNet Carbs

The bright, herby counterpart to the classic red shakshuka — eggs poached in a vivid green sauce of tomatillos, jalapeños, garlic, spinach, and cilantro, finished with crumbled feta. A completely different flavor profile from the classic, and just as good.

Ingredients
·6 large eggs
·8 oz fresh tomatillos, husked and quartered (or 1 can/jar tomatillo salsa)
·2 cups fresh baby spinach
·1 jalapeño, diced
·1 small white onion, diced
·4 cloves garlic, minced
·1/2 cup vegetable or chicken broth
·2 tbsp olive oil
·1 tsp cumin
·Salt and black pepper
·Crumbled feta and fresh cilantro to finish
·Sliced avocado to serve
Instructions
1
Build the Green Sauce
Heat oil over medium. Cook onion and jalapeño 4–5 minutes. Add garlic and cumin, cook 30 seconds. Add tomatillos and broth. Simmer 8–10 minutes until tomatillos soften and break down.
2
Add Spinach
Add spinach and stir until wilted, about 2 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.
3
Blend (Optional)
For a smoother sauce, use an immersion blender to partially blend — leave some texture. Or leave it as-is for a chunkier version.
4
Poach the Eggs
Make 6 wells in the green sauce. Crack one egg per well. Cover the pan and cook over medium-low 5–7 minutes until whites are set but yolks are still runny.
5
Finish & Serve
Scatter crumbled feta and fresh cilantro over the top. Serve with sliced avocado alongside.

Notes

Fresh tomatillos (the small green fruits in papery husks) give the best result. Jarred salsa verde is a solid shortcut.
The flavor profile here is Mexico meets the Middle East — bright, tart, herby, and completely distinct from the classic red version.
If you can't find tomatillos, substitute 1 cup each of green salsa and chicken broth.
