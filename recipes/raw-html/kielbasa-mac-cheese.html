Kielbasa 'Mac & Cheese'

⏱ 25 min🍽 4 servings✓ Strict Keto
490Calories
22gProtein
38gFat
9gNet Carbs

All the creamy, cheesy, smoky satisfaction of mac and cheese — with cauliflower florets instead of pasta and sliced kielbasa making it a full dinner. The sauce is cream cheese and sharp cheddar melted into a silky coating. This is the sausage night that Teddy is going to request on repeat.

Ingredients
·1 lb kielbasa, sliced into coins
·1 large head cauliflower, cut into bite-size florets
·4 oz cream cheese, cubed
·1.5 cups sharp cheddar, shredded (divided)
·1/2 cup heavy cream
·1/2 cup chicken broth
·3 cloves garlic, minced
·1 tbsp butter
·1/2 tsp smoked paprika
·1/2 tsp mustard powder
·Salt and black pepper
·Sliced scallions to garnish
Instructions
1
Steam the Cauliflower
Steam cauliflower florets until just tender, 5–6 minutes. They should still have some bite — they'll cook more in the sauce. Don't overcook or they'll get mushy.
2
Sear the Kielbasa
In a large oven-safe skillet, sear kielbasa coins in butter over medium-high 2–3 minutes per side until caramelized. Remove and set aside.
3
Make the Cheese Sauce
In the same pan, add garlic and cook 30 seconds. Add cream and broth. Add cream cheese and stir over medium-low until melted and smooth. Season with paprika, mustard powder, salt, and pepper.
4
Combine
Add cauliflower to the sauce and toss to coat. Stir in 1 cup of cheddar until melted. Return kielbasa to the pan and mix everything together.
5
Broil
Top with remaining 1/2 cup cheddar. Broil 3–4 minutes until bubbly and golden. Garnish with scallions and serve directly from the pan.

Notes

Mustard powder in cheese sauce adds the same brightness as the mustard you'd put on a hot dog with kielbasa — it's subtle but it's there.
Don't overcook the cauliflower before the sauce — it continues cooking in the cheese sauce and under the broiler.
This is the keto dish that most successfully mimics a classic comfort food. Expect it to become a household staple.
For extra texture: panfry some kielbasa until very crispy and scatter on top after broiling.
