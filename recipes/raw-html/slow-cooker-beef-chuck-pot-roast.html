Slow Cooker Beef Chuck Pot Roast

⏱ 8–9 hrs (slow cooker) 🍽 6 servings ✓ Strict Keto
490Calories
44gProtein
32gFat
7gNet Carbs

A chuck roast braised low and slow all day in beef broth with garlic, rosemary, thyme, and low-carb root vegetables until it's fork-tender and falling apart in a deeply rich, savory braise.

Ingredients
Chuck Roast
·3–4 lb chuck roast
·1 tbsp olive oil (for searing)
·1.5 tsp salt
·1 tsp black pepper
·1 tsp garlic powder
·1 tsp onion powder
Slow Cooker
·1.5 cups beef broth
·1 cup dry red wine (or more broth)
·4 cloves garlic, smashed
·3 sprigs fresh rosemary
·4 sprigs fresh thyme
·2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
·2 tbsp tomato paste
·1 cup turnips, cubed (keto-friendly)
·1 cup celery root (celeriac), cubed
·2 medium parsnips, cubed (use sparingly — slightly higher carb)
Sides
·Cauliflower mash (cauliflower, butter, cream, salt)
·1 lb Brussels sprouts, halved and roasted
Instructions
1
Season & Sear
Pat chuck roast completely dry. Season all sides generously with salt, pepper, garlic powder, and onion powder. Heat oil in a large skillet over high heat. Sear roast 3–4 minutes per side until deeply browned on all sides. This step dramatically deepens the final flavor — don't skip it.
2
Set Up Slow Cooker
Whisk together beef broth, red wine, Worcestershire, and tomato paste. Pour into the slow cooker. Add garlic, rosemary, and thyme. Add cubed vegetables around the edges.
3
Add the Roast
Place seared chuck roast on top of the vegetables and liquid. The liquid should come up about halfway — not fully cover the meat.
4
Cook All Day
Cook on LOW for 8–9 hours or HIGH for 4–5 hours. The roast is done when it falls apart easily when tested with a fork.
5
Shred & Make Gravy
Remove roast and shred or slice. Strain the cooking liquid into a saucepan. Simmer over medium heat 8–10 minutes to reduce into a rich gravy. Taste and adjust seasoning.
6
Make Cauliflower Mash & Brussels
Steam cauliflower until tender, blend with butter, cream, and salt until silky. Roast Brussels sprouts at 425°F for 20 minutes. Plate pot roast over cauliflower mash with gravy, Brussels alongside.

Notes

Searing the chuck roast before the slow cooker is the single most impactful step — the Maillard reaction creates complexity that slow cooking alone can't replicate.
Chuck roast is ideal for slow cooking: the high collagen content melts into gelatin that makes the braise incredibly rich.
Turnips and celeriac are excellent keto-friendly root vegetables for the braise. Parsnips add a touch more carbs but also sweetness — use in moderation.
The cooking liquid reduced to a gravy is extraordinary — make extra and freeze it for future meals.
