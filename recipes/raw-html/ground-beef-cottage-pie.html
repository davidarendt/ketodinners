Ground Beef Cottage Pie

⏱ 40 min🍽 6 servings✓ Strict Keto
430Calories
34gProtein
27gFat
8gNet Carbs

A keto cottage pie with all the depth of the original — spiced ground beef with Worcestershire, garlic, and beef broth — topped with silky cauliflower mash and baked until the top is golden and the filling is bubbling. Hearty British-American comfort in a baking dish.

Ingredients
·1.5 lbs ground beef (80/20)
·1 small yellow onion, diced
·2 carrots, small dice (modest amount for flavor)
·3 cloves garlic, minced
·2 tbsp tomato paste
·1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
·1/2 cup beef broth
·1 tsp dried thyme
·Salt and black pepper
·1 tbsp olive oil
·1 large head cauliflower (for mash)
·4 tbsp butter
·3 tbsp heavy cream
·1/2 cup grated parmesan
·Salt, white pepper, nutmeg
Instructions
1
Make the Mash
Steam cauliflower until very tender. Drain and press dry. Blend with butter, cream, parmesan, salt, white pepper, and nutmeg until silky smooth. Set aside.
2
Brown the Beef
Brown ground beef in olive oil over high heat until well caramelized. Remove; drain fat.
3
Build the Filling
Cook onion and carrot 5 minutes. Add garlic, tomato paste — cook 1 minute. Return beef. Add Worcestershire, broth, thyme. Simmer 10 minutes until most liquid is absorbed. Season well.
4
Assemble
Transfer beef filling to a baking dish. Dollop and spread cauliflower mash evenly over the top. Use a fork to create ridges — they'll brown and crisp beautifully.
5
Bake
Bake at 400°F for 20–25 minutes until mash is golden on the ridges and filling is bubbling at the edges. Rest 5 minutes before serving.

Notes

Pressing the cauliflower completely dry before blending is the single most important step for a fluffy, non-watery mash.
The fork ridges on top are functional, not just decorative — they crisp up and add textural contrast.
Tomato paste cooked directly in the pan before adding liquid ('pinching' the paste) develops a richer, less acidic flavor.
