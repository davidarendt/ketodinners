Andouille with Braised Collard Greens

⏱ 25 min🍽 4 servings✓ Strict Keto
380Calories
17gProtein
28gFat
7gNet Carbs

Andouille rendered until caramelized, then collard greens braised low in garlicky chicken broth with a hit of red pepper flakes until silky and yielding. Southern soul food, keto edition — hearty, deeply satisfying, and completely different from every other sausage night on the plan.

Ingredients
Skillet
·1 lb andouille sausage, sliced into coins
·1 large bunch collard greens, stems removed, leaves sliced into ribbons
·1 small yellow onion, sliced
·4 cloves garlic, minced
·1 cup chicken broth
·1 tbsp olive oil
·1/2 tsp red pepper flakes
·1 tsp apple cider vinegar
·Salt and black pepper
Serve With
·2 bags cauliflower rice, cooked
Instructions
1
Render the Andouille
Heat a large skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high. Add andouille coins and cook 3–4 minutes per side until caramelized and fat rendered. Remove to a plate.
2
Cook Onion & Garlic
Add olive oil and onion to the rendered fat. Cook 4–5 minutes until softened and golden. Add garlic and red pepper flakes, cook 30 seconds.
3
Add Collard Greens
Add collard green ribbons in batches, tossing as they wilt down. Once all greens are in, pour in chicken broth.
4
Braise
Bring to a simmer, reduce heat to medium-low. Cover and cook 10–15 minutes until greens are tender and silky. Stir occasionally.
5
Finish & Serve
Return andouille to the pan. Add apple cider vinegar — it brightens the pot likker and balances the richness. Season with salt and pepper. Serve over cauliflower rice.

Notes

Collard greens are one of the most keto-friendly greens: low carb, high fiber, and they hold up to braising without falling apart.
The apple cider vinegar at the end is essential — Southern cooks have added it to braised greens for generations, and it cuts through the richness beautifully.
The braising liquid (pot likker) is extraordinary — spoon it generously over the cauliflower rice.
Remove the tough center stem from each leaf before slicing — it's too fibrous even after cooking.
