Ground Chicken Thai Basil Stir-Fry

⏱ 20 min🍽 4 servings✓ Strict Keto
360Calories
36gProtein
20gFat
6gNet Carbs

The same Pad Kra Pao technique as the ground turkey version — screaming-hot wok, fish sauce, garlic, chili, and a generous handful of Thai basil — but with ground chicken. Topped with a crispy blistered fried egg. Twenty minutes, maximum flavor.

Ingredients
Stir Fry
·1.25 lbs ground chicken
·1 cup fresh Thai basil (or regular basil), packed
·4 cloves garlic, minced
·3 Thai bird's eye chilies, minced (or 1 jalapeño)
·2 tbsp fish sauce
·1 tbsp oyster sauce
·1 tsp soy sauce
·1/2 tsp white pepper
·2 tbsp avocado oil
Fried Eggs & Serve
·4 large eggs, fried blistered-crispy in hot oil
·2 bags cauliflower rice, cooked
·Lime wedges
·Extra fish sauce at the table
Instructions
1
Prep Everything
Mix fish sauce, oyster sauce, and soy sauce in a bowl. Have basil, garlic, and chilies ready — stir-fry moves fast.
2
Fry the Eggs
Heat a generous amount of oil in a wok until smoking. Crack eggs into hot oil. The edges should blister and crisp within 30 seconds. Cook 1–2 minutes, basting the top with oil. Remove and set aside.
3
Cook the Chicken
Add more oil to the same wok over high heat. Add garlic and chilies — toss 15 seconds. Add ground chicken, break apart. Cook over HIGH heat 4–5 minutes until browned with crispy edges.
4
Add Sauce
Pour sauce over chicken. Toss over high heat 1–2 minutes until sauce coats the meat and caramelizes slightly. Season with white pepper.
5
Add Basil & Serve
Remove from heat. Add Thai basil and toss — the residual heat wilts it perfectly. Plate over cauliflower rice with a crispy egg on top. Serve with lime.

Notes

Ground chicken is slightly more neutral than ground turkey in this application — both work beautifully with the bold Thai sauce.
The blistered fried egg is essential — it's the textural and visual centerpiece of the dish.
High heat is the most important factor. A medium flame will give you steamed, pale chicken instead of caramelized, deeply flavored stir-fry.
Thai basil has an anise-clove note different from Italian basil. Find it at Asian groceries for the authentic version.
