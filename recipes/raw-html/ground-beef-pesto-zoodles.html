Ground Beef Pesto Zoodles

⏱ 20 min🍽 4 servings✓ Strict Keto
450Calories
36gProtein
30gFat
7gNet Carbs

Ground beef browned crispy and deeply caramelized, then tossed with vibrant basil pesto, burst cherry tomatoes, and freshly grated parmesan over zucchini noodles. Bright, fresh, Italian-inspired — ground beef in its best Monday format.

Ingredients
Beef & Sauce
·1.25 lbs ground beef (80/20)
·1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
·1/3 cup basil pesto (store-bought or homemade)
·1/4 cup grated parmesan (plus more to serve)
·3 cloves garlic, minced
·2 tbsp olive oil
·Salt and black pepper
·Red pepper flakes to taste
Zucchini Noodles
·4 medium zucchini, spiralized
·1 tbsp olive oil
·Pinch of salt
·Fresh basil leaves to garnish
Instructions
1
Brown the Beef
Heat a large skillet over high heat. Add ground beef — do NOT stir for 3–4 minutes. Let a dark, crispy crust form on the bottom. Then break up and cook through. Season with salt and pepper. Remove to a plate.
2
Cook Cherry Tomatoes
In the same pan, add olive oil and cherry tomatoes. Cook over medium-high 3–4 minutes until they burst and release their juices. Add garlic and cook 30 seconds.
3
Combine Beef & Tomatoes
Return beef to the pan with the burst tomatoes. Remove from heat. Stir in pesto — adding it off heat preserves the fresh basil flavor.
4
Quick-Cook Zoodles
In a separate pan over medium-high, toss zucchini noodles in olive oil and salt. Cook 2–3 minutes just until tender but with bite. Don't overcook.
5
Toss & Serve
Add zoodles to the beef pan and toss to combine. Add parmesan and toss again. Taste and adjust with salt, pepper flakes, and more pesto if needed. Serve immediately with extra parmesan and fresh basil.

Notes

The crispy crust on the beef is the star of this dish — resist stirring for the first few minutes.
Add pesto off heat to preserve its bright green color and fresh basil flavor.
Zucchini noodles release water as they sit — eat immediately after tossing, or keep the beef and zoodles separate until serving.
Store-bought pesto works well here. Look for one with olive oil rather than seed oils as the base.
