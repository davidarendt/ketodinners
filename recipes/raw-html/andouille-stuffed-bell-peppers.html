Andouille-Stuffed Bell Peppers

⏱ 30 min 🍽 4 servings ✓ Strict Keto
380Calories
18gProtein
26gFat
10gNet Carbs

Bell peppers loaded with a savory filling of diced andouille, cauliflower rice, diced tomatoes, and melted cheddar — a weeknight stuffed pepper that packs serious Cajun heat.

Ingredients
Stuffed Peppers
·4 large bell peppers (any color), tops cut, seeds removed
·12 oz andouille sausage, diced small
·1.5 cups cauliflower rice
·1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes, drained
·1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese
·3 cloves garlic, minced
·1/2 small onion, diced
·1 tsp Cajun seasoning
·1/2 tsp smoked paprika
·1 tbsp olive oil
·Salt and pepper
Side Salad
·4 cups romaine lettuce
·2 tbsp olive oil
·1 tbsp ranch seasoning mixed with 2 tbsp mayo and 2 tbsp water
Instructions
1
Preheat Oven
Preheat oven to 375°F. Place prepared bell pepper shells cut-side up in a baking dish. Brush lightly with olive oil inside and season with a pinch of salt.
2
Cook the Filling
Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium-high. Add andouille and cook 3–4 minutes until browned. Add onion and garlic, cook 2 minutes. Add cauliflower rice and toss to combine.
3
Season & Add Tomatoes
Add drained diced tomatoes, Cajun seasoning, and smoked paprika. Stir and cook 3–4 minutes until cauliflower is tender and liquid mostly absorbed. Taste and adjust seasoning.
4
Fill the Peppers
Spoon filling generously into each pepper shell, pressing down slightly to pack it in. Top each with a generous mound of shredded cheddar.
5
Bake
Bake at 375°F for 22–25 minutes until peppers are tender and cheese is melted, bubbly, and slightly browned. Let rest 5 minutes before serving.

Notes

Draining the canned tomatoes well is important — excess liquid makes the filling watery.
You can prep the filling the night before and refrigerate — just stuff and bake when ready.
The pepper tops can be diced and added to the filling so nothing goes to waste.
Andouille seasoning varies by brand — taste the filling before adding any extra salt.
