Slow Cooker Andouille & Cauliflower Chowder

⏱ 6–7 hrs (hands-off)🍽 6 servings✓ Strict Keto
410Calories
18gProtein
34gFat
9gNet Carbs

A thick, smoky, deeply satisfying chowder that slow-cooks all day — andouille sausage, cauliflower, cream cheese, and cheddar building into a rich soup that eats like a full meal. Sausage night has never been this cozy.

Ingredients
Slow Cooker
·1 lb andouille sausage, sliced into coins
·1 large head cauliflower, cut into small florets
·1 medium yellow onion, diced
·4 cloves garlic, minced
·1 red bell pepper, diced
·3 cups chicken broth
·1 tsp Cajun seasoning
·1/2 tsp smoked paprika
·1/2 tsp black pepper
·Salt to taste
Stir In at the End
·4 oz full-fat cream cheese, cubed and softened
·1 cup heavy cream
·1.5 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese
Garnish
·Sliced scallions
·Extra shredded cheddar
·Sour cream
·Crumbled bacon (optional)
Instructions
1
Optional Sear
For extra depth, sear andouille slices in a hot skillet 2 minutes per side until caramelized. Skip if short on time — the slow cooker still delivers.
2
Load the Slow Cooker
Add sausage, cauliflower, onion, garlic, bell pepper, chicken broth, Cajun seasoning, paprika, and pepper to the slow cooker. Stir to combine.
3
Cook
Cook on LOW for 6–7 hours or HIGH for 3–4 hours, until cauliflower is completely tender.
4
Mash Some Cauliflower
Use a potato masher or the back of a spoon to roughly mash some of the cauliflower directly in the pot — this thickens the broth naturally while keeping some chunks intact.
5
Stir in Cream Cheese & Cream
Add cubed cream cheese and heavy cream. Stir well until cream cheese fully melts into the soup, about 10 minutes on HIGH or just stir until dissolved.
6
Add Cheese & Serve
Stir in shredded cheddar. Taste and adjust seasoning. Ladle into bowls and top with scallions, extra cheese, and sour cream.

Notes

Mashing roughly half the cauliflower is the trick to a thick, creamy chowder without flour or cornstarch.
Add the cream cheese and cream at the end only — dairy added too early can break or curdle.
This is extremely sausage-forward — perfect for your andouille Tuesday slot.
Leftovers thicken significantly overnight. Thin with a splash of broth when reheating.
