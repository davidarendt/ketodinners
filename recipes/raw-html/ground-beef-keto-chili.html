Ground Beef Keto Chili

⏱ 30 min🍽 6 servings✓ Strict Keto
430Calories
36gProtein
26gFat
9gNet Carbs

Rich, deeply spiced ground beef chili with poblanos, bell peppers, crushed tomatoes, and a blend of chili powders — no beans, no filler, nothing to distract from the beef. Topped with sour cream, cheddar, and avocado. This is Monday done right.

Ingredients
Chili
·2 lbs ground beef (80/20)
·2 poblano peppers, diced
·1 red bell pepper, diced
·1 yellow onion, diced
·4 cloves garlic, minced
·1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
·1/2 cup beef broth
·2 tbsp chili powder
·1 tbsp cumin
·1 tsp smoked paprika
·1 tsp oregano
·1/2 tsp cayenne
·1/2 tsp coriander
·1 tsp salt
·2 tbsp olive oil
Toppings
·Full-fat sour cream
·Shredded sharp cheddar
·Sliced avocado or guacamole
·Sliced jalapeño
·Lime wedges
·Fresh cilantro
Instructions
1
Brown the Beef
Heat a large Dutch oven or pot over high heat. Add ground beef in two batches — don't crowd. Cook without stirring 3–4 minutes until a deep brown crust forms on the bottom. Break up and cook through. Remove to a plate and drain excess fat.
2
Sauté Vegetables
Add olive oil, onion, and peppers to the same pot. Cook over medium-high 5–6 minutes until softened and starting to brown. Add garlic and cook 1 minute.
3
Bloom the Spices
Add all spices directly to the vegetables. Stir and cook 30 seconds until fragrant and slightly darkened — this is the blooming step that makes chili taste complex.
4
Add Tomatoes & Broth
Return beef to the pot. Add crushed tomatoes and beef broth. Stir everything together. Bring to a simmer.
5
Simmer
Simmer uncovered over medium-low for 15–20 minutes until chili thickens and flavors deepen. Taste and adjust seasoning — add more cayenne for heat, or a splash of apple cider vinegar to brighten.
6
Serve
Ladle into bowls and top with sour cream, shredded cheddar, avocado, and cilantro.

Notes

Browning the beef in batches rather than crowding the pan is the single most impactful step — you want crust, not steam.
Blooming the spices in oil before adding liquid releases their fat-soluble aromatics and makes a night-and-day difference.
No beans keeps this strictly keto — the beef-to-vegetable ratio more than makes up for it.
This is actually better the next day. Make a double batch and freeze half.
