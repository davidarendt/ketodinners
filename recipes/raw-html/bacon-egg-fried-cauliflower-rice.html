Bacon & Egg Fried Cauliflower Rice

⏱ 20 min 🍽 4 servings ✓ Strict Keto
360Calories
21gProtein
28gFat
7gNet Carbs

Crispy bacon, jammy scrambled eggs, and riced cauliflower stir-fried in soy and sesame oil with scallions and a drizzle of chili oil. All the satisfaction of takeout fried rice, zero the carbs.

Ingredients
Fried Rice
·1 large head cauliflower, riced (or 2 bags frozen cauli rice)
·6 strips thick-cut bacon, cut into lardons
·5 large eggs
·3 tbsp soy sauce (or coconut aminos)
·1 tbsp sesame oil
·1 tbsp avocado oil
·3 scallions, thinly sliced
·3 cloves garlic, minced
·1 tsp ginger, grated
·White pepper to taste
Sesame Cucumber Salad
·2 cucumbers, thinly sliced
·1 tbsp rice vinegar
·1 tbsp sesame oil
·1 tsp sesame seeds
·Pinch of salt and chili flakes
Instructions
1
Prep Cauliflower Rice
If using fresh cauliflower, pulse florets in a food processor until rice-sized. Spread on a baking sheet and pat dry — removing moisture is critical for frying, not steaming.
2
Cook the Bacon
Heat a large wok or skillet over medium-high. Add bacon and cook until crispy. Remove to a plate, leaving the fat in the pan.
3
Fry the Cauliflower
Raise heat to high. Add avocado oil. Add cauliflower rice in a single layer. Do not stir for 2–3 minutes — let it brown. Stir and cook another 2 minutes. Add garlic and ginger, stir 30 seconds.
4
Add Eggs
Push cauliflower to the sides. Add a drizzle of oil to the center and add beaten eggs. Scramble softly in the center, then fold into the cauliflower before eggs are fully set.
5
Season & Finish
Add soy sauce and sesame oil. Toss everything together including bacon. Taste and adjust seasoning with white pepper and more soy if needed.
6
Serve
Plate into bowls and top with sliced scallions. Serve sesame cucumber salad alongside.

Notes

Dry cauliflower is the most important step — wet cauli steams and turns mushy instead of frying.
High heat is essential. A screaming-hot wok or cast iron is far better than a nonstick over low heat.
Add the eggs slightly underdone — they finish cooking from the residual heat of the cauliflower.
A drizzle of chili oil on top before serving elevates this dish significantly.
