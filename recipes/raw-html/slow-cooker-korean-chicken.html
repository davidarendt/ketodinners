Slow Cooker Korean Chicken

⏱ 6–7 hrs (hands-off)🍽 6 servings✓ Strict Keto
400Calories
42gProtein
20gFat
7gNet Carbs

Chicken thighs braised all day in a bold, rich gochujang and soy sauce with sesame, garlic, and ginger — the flavors of Korean BBQ translated into a hands-off slow cooker braise. Shredded and served over cauliflower rice with sesame cucumber and scallions.

Ingredients
·2.5 lbs bone-in skin-on chicken thighs
·3 tbsp gochujang (Korean chili paste)
·3 tbsp soy sauce or coconut aminos
·2 tbsp sesame oil
·1 tbsp rice vinegar
·4 cloves garlic, minced
·1 tbsp ginger, grated
·1 tbsp monk fruit sweetener
·1/2 cup chicken broth
·Sesame seeds and sliced scallions to serve
·2 bags cauliflower rice, cooked
Instructions
1
Mix the Sauce
Whisk together gochujang, soy sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, garlic, ginger, sweetener, and chicken broth.
2
Load the Slow Cooker
Place chicken thighs in the slow cooker. Pour sauce over the top, turning to coat.
3
Cook
Cook on LOW 6–7 hours or HIGH 3–4 hours until chicken is completely tender and falling off the bone.
4
Shred & Reduce
Remove chicken and shred, discarding skin and bones. Skim fat from cooking liquid. Pour liquid into a saucepan and reduce over medium-high heat 5–8 minutes until it's a thick, glossy sauce.
5
Glaze & Serve
Toss shredded chicken with the reduced sauce. Pile over cauliflower rice. Top with sesame seeds and scallions.

Notes

Gochujang is a Korean fermented chili paste — find it at any Asian grocery or most regular supermarkets now. It's essential; sriracha doesn't substitute well.
Reducing the cooking liquid to a glaze is the step that makes this great instead of just good — don't skip it.
The heat level is moderate from the gochujang — add more if you like it spicy, or use less for a milder version.
