Slow Cooker Pork Carnitas

⏱ 8–9 hrs (hands-off)🍽 8 servings✓ Strict Keto
420Calories
38gProtein
29gFat
4gNet Carbs

Pork shoulder slow-cooked all day in citrus, cumin, and garlic until fall-apart tender, then crisped under the broiler so you get that irresistible combination of juicy interior and crackling caramelized crust. Served in lettuce cups with all the fixings.

Ingredients
Pork & Seasoning
·3.5–4 lb boneless pork shoulder, cut into 4 large chunks
·1.5 tsp salt
·1 tsp black pepper
·1.5 tsp cumin
·1 tsp oregano
·1 tsp chili powder
·1/2 tsp smoked paprika
·4 cloves garlic, minced
·1 orange, juiced and zested (just the zest on keto — limit juice to 2 tbsp)
·2 tbsp lime juice
·2 tbsp olive oil
Serve In / With
·1 large head butter lettuce, leaves separated
·2 ripe avocados, diced
·1 cup pico de gallo (fresh tomato, onion, cilantro, jalapeño, lime)
·Full-fat sour cream
·Shredded cheese
·Lime wedges
Instructions
1
Season the Pork
Mix salt, pepper, cumin, oregano, chili powder, and paprika. Rub all over pork chunks. Sear in olive oil over high heat in a cast iron skillet 2–3 minutes per side until browned — this step is worth the 10 minutes for depth of flavor.
2
Slow Cook
Transfer seared pork to slow cooker. Add garlic, orange zest, 2 tbsp orange juice, and lime juice. Cook on LOW 8–9 hours or HIGH 4–5 hours until completely tender and falling apart.
3
Shred & Reserve Liquid
Remove pork and shred with two forks. Skim fat from cooking liquid. Toss shredded pork with a few spoonfuls of the cooking juices to keep it moist.
4
Broil to Crisp
Spread shredded pork in a single layer on a rimmed baking sheet. Broil 5–7 minutes until edges are crispy and caramelized. Watch closely. This step transforms good carnitas into great carnitas.
5
Serve
Load into butter lettuce cups with avocado, pico, sour cream, cheese, and a squeeze of lime.

Notes

The broiler finish is non-negotiable — it's the difference between pulled pork and true carnitas.
Using all the orange juice would add significant carbs; the zest gives all the flavor with almost none of the sugar.
Pork shoulder (aka pork butt) is the cheapest cut for this application and produces the richest result.
This feeds 8 generously — freeze half in the cooking juices for a future 10-minute meal.
