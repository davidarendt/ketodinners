Creamy Thai Green Chicken Curry

⏱ 30 min🍽 4 servings✓ Strict Keto
480Calories
36gProtein
34gFat
8gNet Carbs

Chicken thighs simmered in a fragrant coconut milk green curry sauce with Thai basil, zucchini, and bell pepper — bold, creamy, and aromatic. The kind of meal that makes your kitchen smell like your favorite Thai restaurant. Done in 30 minutes.

Ingredients
Curry
·1.5 lbs boneless chicken thighs, cut into 1.5" chunks
·2–3 tbsp green curry paste (Mae Ploy or Maesri brand)
·1 can (13.5 oz) full-fat coconut milk
·1/2 cup chicken broth
·2 medium zucchini, cut into half-moons
·1 red bell pepper, sliced
·3 cloves garlic, minced
·1 tbsp fish sauce
·1 tsp monk fruit sweetener or erythritol
·1 kaffir lime leaf (optional)
·1 tbsp coconut oil or avocado oil
Finish & Serve
·1 cup fresh Thai basil leaves
·1 lime, juiced
·2 bags cauliflower rice, cooked
·Extra lime wedges
·Sliced red chili or chili flakes for heat
Instructions
1
Bloom the Curry Paste
Heat oil in a large skillet or wok over medium. Add green curry paste and cook, stirring constantly, 1–2 minutes until paste is fragrant and slightly darkened. This step is critical — it activates the aromatics in the paste.
2
Add Coconut Milk
Pour in coconut milk and broth. Stir to combine. Add kaffir lime leaf if using. Bring to a gentle simmer.
3
Add Chicken
Add chicken thigh pieces. Simmer over medium 8–10 minutes until chicken is just cooked through.
4
Add Vegetables
Add zucchini and bell pepper. Simmer 5 more minutes until vegetables are tender but still have bite.
5
Season
Add fish sauce, sweetener, and lime juice. Taste and adjust — the balance should be savory, slightly sweet, and bright from the lime.
6
Add Basil & Serve
Remove from heat and stir in fresh basil. Serve immediately over cauliflower rice with extra lime wedges and chili.

Notes

Mae Ploy or Maesri green curry paste are both superior to most Western supermarket brands — look for them at any Asian grocery.
Full-fat coconut milk is essential for keto — the fat content is the point, and it makes the sauce properly rich.
Blooming the curry paste in oil before adding liquid unlocks the fat-soluble aromatics that make it taste complex rather than raw.
Fish sauce is the salt of Thai cooking — don't substitute soy sauce. The flavor is different and essential.
