Slow Cooker French Onion Chicken

⏱ 6–7 hrs (hands-off)🍽 6 servings✓ Strict Keto
430Calories
40gProtein
25gFat
7gNet Carbs

All the incredible flavors of French onion soup — caramelized onions, beef broth, thyme, and Gruyère — surrounding bone-in chicken thighs in the slow cooker. When you pull the lid, it smells like Paris. Broil the cheese on top at the end for the full effect.

Ingredients
·2.5 lbs bone-in skin-on chicken thighs
·3 large yellow onions, thinly sliced
·4 cloves garlic, minced
·1 cup beef broth
·1/2 cup dry white wine (or more broth)
·1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
·1 tsp fresh thyme (or 1/2 tsp dried)
·1 tbsp butter
·1 tbsp olive oil
·Salt and black pepper
·1.5 cups Gruyère cheese, shredded
Instructions
1
Sauté the Onions (Critical Step)
Cook sliced onions in butter and olive oil over medium-low heat in a large skillet for 20–25 minutes, stirring occasionally, until deeply caramelized and golden brown. Do not rush this — it's the entire flavor base of the dish.
2
Load the Slow Cooker
Add caramelized onions, garlic, beef broth, wine, Worcestershire, and thyme to the slow cooker. Nestle chicken thighs on top, skin-side up. Season with salt and pepper.
3
Cook
Cook on LOW 6–7 hours until chicken is completely tender.
4
Top with Cheese & Broil
Transfer thighs to an oven-safe dish or baking sheet. Spoon caramelized onions over each thigh. Top with a generous mound of Gruyère. Broil 3–4 minutes until cheese is melted, bubbly, and starting to brown.
5
Serve
Serve over cauliflower mash or with roasted vegetables, with extra onion-broth spooned alongside.

Notes

The 20-25 minutes caramelizing the onions before they go in the slow cooker is not optional — raw onions in the slow cooker turn sweet but never develop the deep flavor that properly caramelized onions have.
Gruyère is the correct cheese here — it melts perfectly and has the nutty depth that complements the onions. Swiss works as a substitute.
The braising liquid is extraordinary as a sauce — reduce it on the stovetop for 5 minutes for an incredible gravy.
