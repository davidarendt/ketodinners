Pan-Seared Lamb Chops with Herb Butter

⏱ 20 min🍽 4 servings✓ Strict Keto
520Calories
42gProtein
38gFat
2gNet Carbs

Loin lamb chops seared hard in a screaming-hot cast iron until the fat cap is rendered and the surface has a deep mahogany crust, then basted in rosemary-garlic butter. Fast, impressive, and keto-perfect — lamb chops are essentially pure fat and protein.

Ingredients
·8 lamb loin chops (about 1" thick)
·3 tbsp butter
·3 cloves garlic, smashed
·3 sprigs fresh rosemary
·2 sprigs fresh thyme
·2 tbsp avocado oil
·Flaky sea salt and coarse black pepper
·Lemon wedges to serve
Instructions
1
Prep & Season
Remove lamb from fridge 20 minutes before cooking. Pat completely dry. Season very generously with salt and coarse pepper on both sides.
2
Heat the Pan
Heat cast iron over very high heat until smoking. Add avocado oil.
3
Sear the Fat Cap
Hold each chop upright and sear the fat cap against the hot pan for 1–2 minutes until rendered and golden. This step ensures the fat is crispy rather than rubbery.
4
Sear the Sides
Lay chops flat. Sear 2 minutes per side without moving for medium-rare (130–135°F internal).
5
Baste with Herb Butter
Add butter, garlic, rosemary, and thyme to the pan. Baste continuously with the foaming butter for 60–90 seconds.
6
Rest & Serve
Rest on a cutting board 5 minutes. Serve with lemon wedges and a drizzle of the herb-infused butter from the pan.

Notes

Rendering the fat cap standing upright is the pro technique that elevates lamb chops from good to great.
Lamb chops are expensive but there's very little waste — they're nearly pure protein and fat, making them macros-perfect for keto.
Medium-rare (130–135°F) is the sweet spot for lamb. Cooking past 145°F makes them tough and gamey.
These cook so fast (under 10 active minutes) that the 'expensive' factor per serving is the entire meal — no sides needed beyond a simple salad.
