Crustless Quiche Lorraine

⏱ 40 min🍽 6 servings✓ Strict Keto
380Calories
20gProtein
32gFat
4gNet Carbs

A classic quiche — silky egg custard with smoky bacon and melted Gruyère — baked in a buttered dish without the crust. The result is pure, creamy, deeply savory filling with a golden wobbling top. Elegant enough for a dinner party, easy enough for Friday night.

Ingredients
Quiche
·8 large eggs
·1.5 cups heavy cream
·6 strips thick-cut bacon, cut into lardons
·1 cup Gruyère cheese, shredded (or Swiss)
·1 small shallot, finely minced
·1 tbsp butter (for dish)
·1/4 tsp nutmeg
·1/2 tsp salt
·1/4 tsp white pepper
Simple Side Salad
·4 cups frisée or butter lettuce
·2 tbsp olive oil
·1 tbsp white wine vinegar
·Pinch of salt and Dijon mustard
Instructions
1
Preheat & Prep Dish
Preheat oven to 325°F. Butter a 9-inch pie dish or shallow baking dish generously. Set a kettle of water to boil for the water bath.
2
Cook Bacon & Shallot
Cook bacon lardons in a skillet over medium until crispy. Remove with a slotted spoon to paper towels. In the same fat, cook shallot 2–3 minutes until soft. Drain on paper towels.
3
Mix the Custard
Whisk together eggs and heavy cream until thoroughly combined and smooth. Season with nutmeg, salt, and white pepper. Don't over-whisk — you don't want foam.
4
Assemble
Scatter bacon, shallot, and about 3/4 of the Gruyère evenly across the bottom of the buttered dish. Pour egg custard over the top. Scatter remaining cheese on top.
5
Water Bath Bake
Place quiche dish inside a larger roasting pan. Pour boiling water into the outer pan to come halfway up the sides of the quiche dish. Bake 35–40 minutes until the edges are set and the center jiggles gently like Jell-O (not watery).
6
Rest & Serve
Remove from water bath and rest 10 minutes before slicing. Serve warm or at room temperature with a simple dressed salad alongside.

Notes

The water bath ensures the custard cooks gently and evenly — without it, the edges overcook and the center stays raw.
The center should jiggle like set Jell-O when you take it out — it continues to firm up during the 10-minute rest.
Gruyère is the correct cheese for quiche Lorraine. Swiss works in a pinch but has less flavor.
Quiche is excellent at room temperature and reheats beautifully — make it on Friday and enjoy leftovers Saturday morning.
