Smoked Spatchcock Chicken

⏱ 2–3 hrs🍽 4 servings✓ Strict Keto
480Calories
52gProtein
30gFat
1gNet Carbs

A whole chicken spatchcocked flat and slow-smoked until the skin is bronzed and the meat is impossibly juicy. Flattening the bird means it cooks evenly and takes on smoke all over. Plan it the day before.

Ingredients
·1 whole chicken (4–5 lbs), spatchcocked
·2 tbsp olive oil
·1 tbsp smoked paprika
·1 tbsp garlic powder
·1 tbsp onion powder
·1 tsp chili powder
·1 tbsp coarse salt
·1 tsp coarse black pepper
·Wood chunks (hickory or applewood)
Instructions
1
Spatchcock
Using kitchen shears, cut out the backbone and press the chicken flat, breaking the breastbone.
2
Season
Pat dry. Rub all over with olive oil, then coat with the combined paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Rest uncovered in the fridge overnight for the best skin.
3
Set Up the Smoker
Bring the smoker to 250°F with hickory or applewood.
4
Smoke
Smoke skin-side up until the thickest part of the breast reaches 160°F and the thighs 175°F, about 2–3 hours.
5
Rest & Carve
Rest 15 minutes (carryover brings the breast to 165°F), then carve and serve.

Notes

Spatchcocking is the single biggest upgrade for even cooking and crispier skin.
The overnight uncovered fridge rest dries the skin so it bronzes instead of turning rubbery.
Save the backbone and carcass for a rich keto bone broth.
