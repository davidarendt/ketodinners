Andouille Cauliflower Jambalaya

⏱ 25 min🍽 4 servings✓ Strict Keto
400Calories
20gProtein
28gFat
9gNet Carbs

A keto jambalaya that hits every note of the original — andouille, the Cajun holy trinity (onion, celery, bell pepper), Cajun seasoning, and cauliflower rice absorbing all those bold, smoky flavors. Done in one pan, done in 25 minutes.

Ingredients
·1 lb andouille sausage, sliced
·1 large head cauliflower, riced (or 2 bags frozen)
·1 yellow onion, diced
·3 stalks celery, diced
·1 green bell pepper, diced
·4 cloves garlic, minced
·1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes, drained
·1/2 cup chicken broth
·1.5 tsp Cajun seasoning
·1 tsp smoked paprika
·1/2 tsp dried thyme
·1/4 tsp cayenne
·Salt and pepper
·Sliced scallions to serve
·2 tbsp olive oil
Instructions
1
Brown the Andouille
Heat oil in a large cast iron or skillet over medium-high. Sear andouille coins 2–3 minutes per side until caramelized. Remove to a plate.
2
Cook the Trinity
In the same fat, cook onion, celery, and bell pepper over medium-high for 5–6 minutes until softened and starting to char. Add garlic and cook 30 seconds.
3
Bloom the Spices
Add Cajun seasoning, paprika, thyme, and cayenne. Stir 30 seconds until fragrant.
4
Add Tomatoes & Cauliflower
Add drained tomatoes and broth. Stir to combine. Add cauliflower rice. Toss everything together and cook over medium-high 5–6 minutes, stirring occasionally.
5
Finish
Return andouille to the pan. Toss everything together and cook 2 minutes. Taste and adjust seasoning — jambalaya should be bold. Serve topped with scallions.

Notes

The secret to great jambalaya is the fond — the browned bits from the sausage and vegetables that stay in the pan are the entire flavor base.
Riced cauliflower absorbs the Cajun broth and spices beautifully, making this one of the best keto rice substitutes on the plan.
Add a splash more broth if the mixture looks too dry — cauliflower absorbs liquid quickly.
