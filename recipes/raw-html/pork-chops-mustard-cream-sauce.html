Pork Chops with Mustard Cream Pan Sauce

⏱ 30 min 🍽 4 servings ✓ Strict Keto
490Calories
42gProtein
34gFat
4gNet Carbs

Thick-cut pork chops seared in a cast iron until golden, then rested while a silky Dijon and thyme cream sauce is built in the same pan from the fond. Classic technique, dinner-party results.

Ingredients
Pork Chops
·4 bone-in pork chops, 1" thick (about 10–12 oz each)
·1 tsp garlic powder
·1 tsp smoked paprika
·1 tsp salt
·1/2 tsp black pepper
·2 tbsp avocado oil
Mustard Cream Sauce
·2 shallots, finely minced
·3 cloves garlic, minced
·1/2 cup chicken broth
·3/4 cup heavy cream
·2 tbsp whole grain Dijon mustard
·1 tbsp butter
·Fresh thyme (4 sprigs)
·Salt and pepper to taste
Sides
·1 large head broccoli, cut into florets
·2 tbsp olive oil
·Salt, pepper, garlic powder
·4 cups mixed greens, lightly dressed
Instructions
1
Season & Temper
Remove pork chops from fridge 20 minutes before cooking. Pat dry and season with garlic powder, paprika, salt, and pepper on both sides.
2
Sear the Chops
Heat cast iron over high until smoking. Add avocado oil. Sear chops 3–4 minutes per side without moving until deep golden crust forms. Internal temp should be about 145°F. Rest on a plate, tented loosely with foil.
3
Build the Sauce
Reduce heat to medium. Add shallots to the pan drippings. Cook 2 minutes until softened. Add garlic and thyme sprigs, cook 30 seconds.
4
Deglaze
Pour in chicken broth, scraping up all the browned fond from the bottom of the pan — this is the flavor foundation of the sauce. Simmer until reduced by half.
5
Add Cream & Mustard
Pour in heavy cream. Simmer 3–4 minutes until the sauce coats the back of a spoon. Stir in Dijon and butter. Taste and adjust seasoning. Remove thyme sprigs.
6
Serve
Return any accumulated resting juices from the pork to the sauce. Plate chops and spoon sauce generously over them. Serve with roasted broccoli and a light salad.

Notes

Pat the pork chops completely dry — moisture on the surface is the enemy of a proper sear.
Bone-in chops have more flavor and stay juicier than boneless. The bone also protects the meat from overcooking.
145°F internal is perfectly safe and gives a slightly pink, juicy result. Don't cook past 160°F or they'll be dry.
The fond scraped from the cast iron is the entire flavor of the sauce — don't skip the deglazing step.
