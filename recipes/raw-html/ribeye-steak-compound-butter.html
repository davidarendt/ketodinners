Ribeye Steak with Garlic Compound Butter

⏱ 30 min 🍽 2 servings ✓ Strict Keto
640Calories
46gProtein
50gFat
2gNet Carbs

A thick ribeye seared hard in a screaming-hot cast iron and basted in garlic-rosemary compound butter until a deep mahogany crust forms. Once per 6 weeks — make it count.

Ingredients
Steak
·2 ribeye steaks, 1.25" thick (about 14 oz each)
·1 tbsp avocado oil or ghee
·Kosher salt and coarse black pepper
Compound Butter
·4 tbsp unsalted butter, softened
·2 cloves garlic, minced fine
·1 tsp fresh rosemary, minced
·1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
·Pinch of flaky salt
Sides
·1 bunch asparagus, trimmed
·2 tbsp olive oil
·Salt and pepper
·4 cups arugula + 1 tbsp lemon juice + 2 tbsp olive oil
Instructions
1
Make Compound Butter
Mix softened butter with garlic, rosemary, thyme, and flaky salt. Roll in plastic wrap into a log and refrigerate until firm, at least 30 minutes (or make ahead).
2
Prep the Steaks
Remove steaks from fridge 30–45 minutes before cooking. Pat completely dry with paper towels. Season very generously with kosher salt and coarse black pepper on both sides and edges.
3
Preheat the Pan
Heat a cast iron skillet over very high heat for 4–5 minutes until smoking. Add avocado oil.
4
Sear Hard
Lay steaks away from you into the pan. Do not touch for 3 minutes. Flip once — cook 2.5–3 minutes on the second side for medium-rare (130–135°F internal).
5
Baste with Butter
In the last 90 seconds, add 2 slices of compound butter to the pan. Tilt the pan and baste the steak continuously with the foaming butter using a spoon.
6
Rest
Remove steaks to a cutting board and rest uncovered 5–8 minutes. This step is mandatory — do not skip it.
7
Roast Asparagus & Serve
While steak rests, roast asparagus at 425°F for 10 minutes or cook in the still-hot pan 3–4 minutes. Serve steak sliced or whole with remaining compound butter melting over the top.

Notes

The most important step: pat the steak completely dry. Surface moisture = steam, not sear.
Do not move the steak during searing. The crust needs uninterrupted contact with the cast iron.
A thermometer is the only reliable way to hit medium-rare: pull at 130°F, it rises to 135°F while resting.
Compound butter keeps frozen for 2 months — make a double batch and use it on chicken all week.
This is the once-per-6-weeks splurge. Enjoy every bite.
