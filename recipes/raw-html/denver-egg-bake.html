Denver Egg Bake

⏱ 25 min🍽 4 servings✓ Strict Keto
360Calories
26gProtein
27gFat
5gNet Carbs

All the flavors of a Denver omelet — ham, bell pepper, onion, sharp cheddar — baked in a skillet until puffed and golden. Easier than individual omelets, feeds the whole family at once, and comes together in 25 minutes flat.

Ingredients
·8 large eggs
·6 oz diced ham (thick-cut deli ham or leftover ham)
·1 red bell pepper, diced
·1 green bell pepper, diced
·1/2 yellow onion, diced
·1 cup sharp cheddar, shredded (divided)
·3 tbsp heavy cream
·2 tbsp butter
·Salt and black pepper
·Fresh chives or parsley to garnish
Instructions
1
Preheat Broiler
Set broiler to high.
2
Sauté Filling
Melt butter in a 10-inch oven-safe skillet over medium-high. Add peppers and onion and cook 4–5 minutes until softened. Add ham and cook 2 minutes.
3
Add Eggs
Whisk eggs with cream, salt, and pepper. Pour over the filling evenly. Scatter 3/4 cup cheddar over the top. Cook undisturbed over medium-low for 4–5 minutes until edges are set.
4
Broil
Scatter remaining cheddar on top. Transfer to broiler 4–6 inches from heat. Broil 2–3 minutes until top is golden and eggs are fully set.
5
Rest & Serve
Rest 2 minutes. Slice into wedges and garnish with fresh herbs.

Notes

This is the most approachable, family-friendly egg dish on the plan. Teddy will eat this without hesitation.
Any leftover ham works perfectly here — this is a great use for Easter or holiday ham.
The broiler finish is what gives the top that golden, slightly caramelized crust that makes this feel like a restaurant dish.
Swap the ham for cooked bacon or crumbled sausage for easy variations.
