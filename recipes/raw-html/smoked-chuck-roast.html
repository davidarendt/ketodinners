Smoked Chuck Roast (Poor Man's Brisket)

⏱ 6 hrs 🍽 8 servings ✓ Strict Keto
480Calories
46gProtein
32gFat
2gNet Carbs

A chuck roast seasoned with nothing more than salt, pepper, and garlic powder, then smoked low and slow to produce a deeply beefy, bark-covered slab that rivals brisket at a fraction of the price.

Ingredients
Chuck Roast & Rub
·1 chuck roast, 3–4 lbs
·1 tbsp coarse kosher salt
·1 tbsp coarse black pepper
·1 tsp garlic powder
·1 tsp onion powder
·Optional: 1/2 tsp cayenne
Spritz
·1/2 cup beef broth
·1/4 cup water
Serving
·Keto coleslaw (cabbage, mayo, ACV, celery seed)
·Dill pickles
·Sugar-free BBQ sauce (optional)
·Roasted vegetables of choice
Instructions
1
Season the Roast
Mix salt, pepper, garlic powder, and onion powder. Pat the chuck roast dry and season generously on all sides. Let sit at room temp 30 minutes before it goes on the smoker.
2
Fire the Smoker
Heat smoker to 250°F. Use oak or hickory wood. Both pair naturally with beef.
3
Smoke Uncovered
Place chuck roast directly on the smoker grate. Smoke at 250°F for 3 hours, spritzing with the beef broth mixture every 60–90 minutes to keep the surface moist and build bark.
4
Wrap at the Stall
When the internal temperature stalls around 160–165°F (usually around hour 3), wrap the roast tightly in butcher paper or foil. Return to smoker.
5
Pull at 200–205°F
Continue smoking until internal temperature reaches 200–205°F, about 2–3 more hours. The roast is done when a probe slides in with zero resistance.
6
Rest & Slice or Pull
Rest wrapped in a cooler for at least 45 minutes. Slice against the grain for brisket-style cuts, or pull apart for a shredded presentation.

Notes

Chuck roast runs 1/3 the price of brisket and produces an equally impressive result on the smoker.
The bark on a well-smoked chuck roast is remarkable — coarse salt and pepper is all it needs.
Don't skip the rest. The minimum is 45 minutes; an hour or more is better.
Leftovers make incredible eggs the next morning — sauté with onions and top with fried eggs.
