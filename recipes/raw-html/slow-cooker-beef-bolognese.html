Slow Cooker Beef Bolognese

⏱ 7–8 hrs (hands-off)🍽 8 servings✓ Strict Keto
420Calories
32gProtein
26gFat
7gNet Carbs

Bolognese the way it was meant to be made — low and slow all day. Ground beef and pork (optional) simmered with soffritto, white wine, milk, and crushed tomatoes for 7–8 hours until the sauce is impossibly rich, concentrated, and deeply savory. Serve over zucchini noodles.

Ingredients
·1.5 lbs ground beef (80/20)
·1/2 lb ground pork (optional, adds richness)
·1 small yellow onion, finely diced
·2 stalks celery, finely diced
·1 medium carrot, finely grated
·4 cloves garlic, minced
·1/3 cup dry white wine
·1/4 cup whole milk
·1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
·1/2 cup beef broth
·1 tsp dried oregano
·Pinch nutmeg
·Salt and black pepper
·Parmesan to serve
Instructions
1
Brown the Meat
Brown ground beef (and pork if using) in batches over high heat in a skillet until deeply caramelized. Drain fat. This step can't happen in the slow cooker — the browning is essential for flavor.
2
Cook the Soffritto
In the same skillet, cook onion, celery, and carrot over medium-low for 8–10 minutes until very soft. Add garlic and cook 1 minute.
3
Add Wine & Milk
Add white wine to the soffritto and cook 2 minutes. Add milk and cook 2 more minutes. Both fully absorb.
4
Load Slow Cooker
Combine browned meat, soffritto, crushed tomatoes, broth, oregano, and nutmeg in the slow cooker.
5
Cook
Cook on LOW 7–8 hours. The sauce should be very thick and concentrated by the end. Taste and season generously.
6
Serve
Quick-cook zucchini noodles. Plate with a generous ladle of Bolognese and a heavy hand with the parmesan.

Notes

The milk and white wine steps before the slow cooker are the classic Bolognese technique (Marcella Hazan's) and are non-negotiable for the right flavor.
This makes a large batch — freeze half for a future 10-minute weeknight meal.
Ground pork adds significant richness. If skipping it, use 2 lbs ground beef instead.
