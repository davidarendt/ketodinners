Smoked Chicken Thighs

⏱ 2.5 hrs 🍽 4 servings ✓ Strict Keto
420Calories
38gProtein
29gFat
3gNet Carbs

Bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs rubbed in a smoked paprika and garlic spice blend, then smoked at 250°F until the skin is lacquered and the meat is fall-off-the-bone tender.

Ingredients
Chicken & Rub
·4–6 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
·2 tbsp smoked paprika
·1 tbsp garlic powder
·1 tsp onion powder
·1 tsp dried thyme
·1 tsp black pepper
·1 tsp kosher salt
·1/2 tsp cayenne (optional)
·1 tbsp yellow mustard (binder)
Herb Butter
·4 tbsp softened butter
·1 tbsp fresh herbs (parsley, thyme, rosemary)
·1 clove garlic, minced
·Pinch of salt
Sides
·2 lbs mixed vegetables (zucchini, bell pepper, broccoli)
·2 tbsp olive oil, salt, pepper
Instructions
1
Apply Rub
Pat chicken thighs completely dry. Coat with a thin layer of yellow mustard. Mix all rub ingredients and apply generously on all sides and under the skin where possible. Refrigerate uncovered 2–4 hours for best results, or proceed immediately.
2
Fire the Smoker
Heat smoker to 250°F. Use apple or cherry wood for a mild, sweet smoke that complements chicken well. Avoid mesquite — it can overpower poultry.
3
Smoke Skin-Side Up
Place thighs skin-side up on the smoker. Smoke at 250°F for 1.5–2 hours until internal temperature reaches 175°F.
4
Crisp the Skin (Optional)
For crackling-crispy skin: transfer thighs to a hot grill or 450°F oven for 5–8 minutes at the end. This caramelizes the skin without losing the smoky flavor.
5
Make Herb Butter & Roast Veg
While chicken smokes, mix herb butter ingredients. Toss vegetables in olive oil, spread on a baking sheet, roast at 400°F for 25 minutes.
6
Rest & Serve
Rest chicken 10 minutes. Plate with a pat of herb butter melting over each thigh and roasted vegetables alongside.

Notes

175°F is the target for thighs — higher than breast meat (165°F), because the connective tissue in thighs needs more heat to break down into rich gelatin.
Apple and cherry wood both complement poultry beautifully. Hickory works too but is slightly bolder.
Exposing the skin to high heat at the end (grill or oven) is the trick to crispy smoked chicken skin.
This feeds 4 easily — any leftovers are fantastic chopped over salad the next day.
