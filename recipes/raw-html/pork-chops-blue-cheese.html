Pork Chops with Blue Cheese & Roasted Garlic Sauce

⏱ 30 min🍽 4 servings✓ Strict Keto
510Calories
44gProtein
34gFat
4gNet Carbs

Thick-cut pork chops seared in cast iron and finished in a rich blue cheese and roasted garlic cream pan sauce — bold, funky, creamy, and completely different from the mustard cream version that everyone loves. A second pork chop format worth having in the rotation.

Ingredients
·4 bone-in pork chops, 1" thick
·2 tbsp avocado oil
·Salt, pepper, garlic powder
·1 whole head of garlic, roasted (or 4–5 cloves, pan-softened)
·1/2 cup heavy cream
·1/4 cup chicken broth
·3 oz blue cheese (Gorgonzola, Roquefort, or Maytag), crumbled
·1 tbsp butter
·Fresh thyme
·Chives to garnish
Instructions
1
Roast the Garlic Ahead
Slice the top off a head of garlic, drizzle with oil, wrap in foil, and roast at 400°F for 40–45 minutes until completely soft. Squeeze out cloves. (Do this ahead; refrigerate for up to a week.)
2
Sear the Chops
Pat chops dry. Season with salt, pepper, and garlic powder. Heat avocado oil in cast iron over high until smoking. Sear chops 3–4 minutes per side until deep golden. Remove to rest (target 145°F internal).
3
Build the Sauce
Reduce heat to medium. Add butter and roasted garlic cloves — mash them slightly into the pan. Cook 30 seconds.
4
Add Cream
Add broth and cream. Scrape up all the fond. Simmer 3–4 minutes until sauce thickens slightly.
5
Add Blue Cheese & Serve
Remove from heat. Crumble in blue cheese and stir until melted into the sauce. Plate chops and spoon sauce over generously. Garnish with chives.

Notes

Roasted garlic is sweet and mild — completely different from raw garlic. It's worth roasting a few heads at once and keeping them refrigerated.
Blue cheese melts more successfully when added off heat — direct heat can cause it to break.
Gorgonzola (milder, creamier) works better here than very pungent Roquefort if you want a more approachable blue cheese sauce.
This is the pork chop for nights when you want something bold and different from the mustard cream version.
