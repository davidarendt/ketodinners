Buffalo Baked Chicken Thighs

⏱ 35 min🍽 4 servings✓ Strict Keto
430Calories
40gProtein
28gFat
3gNet Carbs

Bone-in chicken thighs baked at high heat until the skin is crackly, then coated in a garlic-butter buffalo sauce and returned to the oven to caramelize into a sticky, spicy-tangy glaze. Served with blue cheese dressing and celery.

Ingredients
Chicken
·4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
·1 tsp baking powder (secret skin-crisping trick)
·1/2 tsp garlic powder
·1/2 tsp salt
·1/4 tsp black pepper
Buffalo Sauce
·1/3 cup Frank's RedHot (or similar cayenne-based hot sauce)
·3 tbsp unsalted butter, melted
·1/2 tsp garlic powder
·Pinch of salt
Blue Cheese Dip & Sides
·1/2 cup mayo
·1/4 cup crumbled blue cheese
·2 tbsp sour cream
·1 tbsp white wine vinegar
·Salt and pepper
·Celery stalks to serve
·4 cups romaine, lightly dressed
Instructions
1
Prep Chicken
Pat chicken thighs completely dry. Mix baking powder, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Dust over both sides — the baking powder is the trick to extra-crispy skin in the oven.
2
Bake High Heat
Place chicken on a wire rack set over a baking sheet (this allows airflow under the skin). Bake at 425°F for 25 minutes until skin is golden and starting to crisp.
3
Make Buffalo Sauce
Whisk together hot sauce, melted butter, and garlic powder.
4
Sauce & Return to Oven
Remove chicken from oven. Brush generously with buffalo sauce on all sides. Return to oven for 8–10 more minutes until sauce is caramelized and sticky.
5
Make Blue Cheese Dip
Mix mayo, blue cheese, sour cream, and white wine vinegar. Season with salt and pepper.
6
Serve
Plate thighs with celery stalks, blue cheese dip, and a simple dressed romaine alongside.

Notes

Baking powder on chicken skin is a restaurant trick — it raises the pH of the skin and helps it brown more deeply and get crispier.
The wire rack is also key — air circulating under the chicken prevents the bottom from steaming and getting soggy.
Frank's RedHot is the classic buffalo sauce base. Most other hot sauces can be substituted.
This is simple enough for a weeknight but impressive enough for having people over.
