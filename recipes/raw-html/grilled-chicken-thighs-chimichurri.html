Grilled Chicken Thighs with Chimichurri

⏱ 25 min 🍽 4 servings ✓ Strict Keto
420Calories
38gProtein
28gFat
4gNet Carbs

Bone-in chicken thighs grilled until the skin is crackly and lacquered, served with a vibrant, herbaceous chimichurri made from parsley, garlic, and red wine vinegar.

Ingredients
Chicken
·4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
·2 tbsp olive oil
·1 tsp garlic powder
·1 tsp smoked paprika
·1 tsp salt
·1/2 tsp black pepper
Chimichurri
·1 cup flat-leaf parsley, packed
·3 cloves garlic
·3 tbsp red wine vinegar
·1/2 cup olive oil
·1/2 tsp chili flakes
·1/2 tsp dried oregano
·1/2 tsp salt
·Black pepper to taste
Sides
·1 bunch asparagus, trimmed
·2 tbsp olive oil, salt and pepper
Instructions
1
Make Chimichurri
Finely chop parsley and garlic by hand or pulse in a food processor until coarsely minced — not a paste. Combine with red wine vinegar, olive oil, chili flakes, oregano, salt, and pepper. Taste and adjust. Make at least 15 minutes ahead so flavors meld.
2
Season Chicken
Pat chicken thighs dry. Coat with olive oil and season with garlic powder, paprika, salt, and pepper.
3
Grill Skin-Side Down
Heat grill to medium-high. Place thighs skin-side down. Cook 5–6 minutes without touching until skin releases and is golden and crispy.
4
Flip & Finish
Flip and cook 10–12 more minutes until internal temperature reaches 165–170°F. Move to cooler part of grill if flare-ups occur.
5
Grill Asparagus
Toss asparagus in olive oil, salt, and pepper. Grill 3–4 minutes until tender with char marks.
6
Serve
Plate chicken and asparagus. Spoon chimichurri generously over everything. Pass extra at the table.

Notes

Chimichurri is best made at least 15 minutes ahead — the acidity mellows and the herbs bloom in the oil.
Hand-chop the chimichurri rather than blending it to a puree — texture is everything here.
Chimichurri keeps refrigerated for 5 days and improves with time. Make a double batch.
This sauce is also excellent on the smoked chicken thighs from earlier in the plan.
