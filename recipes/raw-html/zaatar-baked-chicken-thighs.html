Za'atar Baked Chicken Thighs

⏱ 35 min🍽 4 servings✓ Strict Keto
390Calories
37gProtein
25gFat
3gNet Carbs

Bone-in thighs coated in za'atar — the Middle Eastern blend of thyme, sumac, sesame, and oregano — with olive oil and lemon, then baked at high heat until the skin turns a deep golden-herb crust. Bright, aromatic, effortless.

Ingredients
·4 bone-in skin-on chicken thighs
·3 tbsp za'atar spice blend
·3 tbsp olive oil
·2 cloves garlic, minced
·1 lemon, zested and juiced
·1/2 tsp salt
·1/4 tsp black pepper
Instructions
1
Coat the Chicken
Mix za'atar, olive oil, garlic, lemon zest, salt, and pepper into a paste. Coat thighs all over and under the skin where possible. Let sit 15–30 minutes at room temperature, or marinate overnight.
2
Preheat
Heat oven to 425°F. Place thighs skin-side up on a wire rack set over a rimmed baking sheet — this allows airflow under the skin for crispness.
3
Bake
Bake 30–35 minutes until skin is deeply golden and internal temperature reaches 165–170°F.
4
Finish
Squeeze remaining lemon juice over the hot chicken straight from the oven. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with a simple fattoush or cucumber-tomato salad.

Notes

Za'atar quality varies enormously — look for a brand with sumac in the ingredient list. Penzeys and Spicewalla both make excellent versions.
The wire rack is key — it ensures the bottom skin crisps just as much as the top.
This is the easiest high-impact dinner on the entire list. 5 minutes of prep, 35 minutes of oven.
