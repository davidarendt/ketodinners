Chicken Tikka Masala

⏱ 35 min🍽 4 servings✓ Strict Keto
460Calories
40gProtein
28gFat
9gNet Carbs

Chicken thighs marinated in spiced yogurt, charred under the broiler, then simmered in a tomato cream sauce loaded with garam masala, ginger, and garlic. Faster than delivery, leagues better than most restaurants. Served over cauliflower rice.

Ingredients
Chicken Marinade
·1.5 lbs boneless chicken thighs, cut into 1.5" chunks
·1/2 cup full-fat Greek yogurt
·1 tbsp lemon juice
·1.5 tsp garam masala
·1 tsp cumin
·1 tsp turmeric
·1 tsp smoked paprika
·3 cloves garlic, minced
·1 tsp ginger, grated
·1 tsp salt
Tikka Masala Sauce
·2 tbsp butter or ghee
·1 small onion, finely diced
·4 cloves garlic, minced
·1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
·1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes
·1 tsp garam masala
·1 tsp cumin
·1 tsp coriander
·1/2 tsp turmeric
·1/2 tsp chili powder
·3/4 cup heavy cream
·Salt to taste
·Fresh cilantro to garnish
Instructions
1
Marinate
Mix yogurt, lemon juice, and all marinade spices. Coat chicken pieces thoroughly. Marinate at least 30 minutes at room temperature or up to overnight in the fridge.
2
Broil the Chicken
Spread marinated chicken on a foil-lined baking sheet. Broil 8–10 minutes, turning once, until chicken is slightly charred in spots and just cooked through. The char is crucial for flavor.
3
Make the Sauce
Melt butter in a large skillet over medium. Add onion, cook 5–6 minutes until soft and golden. Add garlic and ginger, cook 1 minute. Add all sauce spices and cook 30 seconds until fragrant.
4
Add Tomatoes & Simmer
Add crushed tomatoes. Simmer 10 minutes until sauce thickens and darkens. Season with salt.
5
Add Cream & Chicken
Stir in heavy cream. Add broiled chicken pieces and any resting juices. Simmer together 5 minutes. Taste and adjust seasoning.
6
Serve
Plate over cauliflower rice and garnish with fresh cilantro.

Notes

The broiler char on the marinated chicken is what makes tikka masala taste like tikka masala — don't skip it.
Garam masala quality varies enormously by brand. Bloom Foods, Spicewalla, and Penzeys are reliable.
Ghee instead of butter adds an authentic richness — worth using if you have it.
This is excellent made the day before — the sauce improves dramatically overnight.
