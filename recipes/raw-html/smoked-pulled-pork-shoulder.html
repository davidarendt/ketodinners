Smoked Pulled Pork Shoulder

⏱ 8–10 hrs 🍽 8 servings ✓ Strict Keto
440Calories
42gProtein
30gFat
3gNet Carbs

A bone-in pork shoulder rubbed in a sugar-free dry rub and smoked low and slow until it pulls apart into tender, smoky strands. The Saturday cook that makes the whole house smell incredible.

Ingredients
Pork & Dry Rub
·1 bone-in pork shoulder / pork butt (7–9 lbs)
·2 tbsp smoked paprika
·1 tbsp garlic powder
·1 tbsp onion powder
·1 tbsp chili powder
·2 tsp cumin
·2 tsp black pepper
·1 tbsp kosher salt
·1 tsp cayenne (optional)
·2 tbsp yellow mustard (binder)
Spritz (every 2 hrs)
·1/2 cup apple cider vinegar
·1/2 cup water
Serving
·Sugar-free BBQ sauce
·Keto coleslaw (cabbage, mayo, ACV, salt, celery seed)
·Dill pickles
Instructions
1
Apply Rub Night Before
Pat pork dry. Coat all sides with a thin layer of yellow mustard — this acts as a binder. Mix together all dry rub ingredients and coat the shoulder generously on all sides. Wrap and refrigerate overnight.
2
Prep the Smoker
Fire up your smoker to 225°F. Use hickory, oak, or apple wood. Allow to come to stable temperature before adding the meat.
3
Start the Smoke
Place pork shoulder fat-cap up on the smoker. Insert a probe thermometer if you have one. Smoke undisturbed for the first 3 hours — this is when the bark forms.
4
Spritz Regularly
Starting at hour 3, spritz the pork with apple cider vinegar and water mixture every 60–90 minutes to keep the surface moist and build bark.
5
Power Through the Stall
Between 160–170°F internal temperature, the pork will stall for 1–2 hours. This is normal. Keep the temperature steady and let it ride. You can wrap in butcher paper at this point to push through faster.
6
Pull at 200–205°F
The pork is done when internal temperature reaches 200–205°F and a probe slides in with no resistance. Total time is typically 8–10 hours.
7
Rest & Pull
Wrap in butcher paper or foil and rest in a cooler (with the pork wrapped) for at least 1 hour — up to 2 hours is ideal. Pull apart with two forks or meat claws. Serve with sugar-free BBQ sauce, coleslaw, and pickles.

Notes

The overnight rub is not optional — it makes a profound difference in bark development and flavor penetration.
Fat-cap up lets the fat baste the meat as it renders down through the bark.
Don't rush the stall — wrapping in butcher paper (Texas crutch) speeds it up without making it mushy.
Leftover pulled pork freezes beautifully — portion and freeze for future quick weeknight meals.
This easily feeds 8 — plan to have leftovers.
