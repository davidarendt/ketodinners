Thai Basil Ground Turkey (Pad Kra Pao)

⏱ 20 min🍽 4 servings✓ Strict Keto
370Calories
34gProtein
22gFat
6gNet Carbs

Thailand's most beloved street food — ground turkey stir-fried at screaming-hot heat with Thai basil, fish sauce, oyster sauce, garlic, and chili until each bite is fragrant, savory, slightly sweet, and addictive. Topped with a crispy fried egg. This is the ground turkey recipe that makes you stop thinking ground turkey is boring.

Ingredients
Stir Fry
·1.25 lbs ground turkey
·1 cup fresh Thai basil leaves (or regular basil, loosely packed)
·4 cloves garlic, minced
·3–4 Thai bird's eye chilies, minced (or 1 jalapeño for less heat)
·2 tbsp fish sauce
·1 tbsp oyster sauce (or coconut aminos + a little fish sauce)
·1 tsp soy sauce
·1/2 tsp white pepper
·2 tbsp avocado oil
Fried Eggs & Serve
·4 large eggs, fried in oil until edges are crispy
·2 bags (12 oz each) cauliflower rice, cooked
·Lime wedges
·Extra fish sauce and chili flakes at the table
Instructions
1
Prep Everything First
Stir-fry moves fast — have all ingredients measured and ready before you turn on the heat. Mix fish sauce, oyster sauce, and soy sauce in a small bowl.
2
Fry the Eggs
In a wok or large skillet, heat a generous amount of oil until smoking. Crack eggs directly into the hot oil. The edges should blister and crisp within 30 seconds. Cook 1–2 minutes until whites are set but yolk is still runny. Remove and set aside.
3
Stir-Fry the Turkey
Add a bit more oil to the same wok over high heat. Add garlic and chilies, toss 15 seconds — don't let them burn. Add ground turkey, breaking up. Cook over HIGH heat 4–5 minutes until browned with some crispy edges.
4
Sauce the Turkey
Pour in the fish sauce mixture. Toss over high heat 1–2 minutes until sauce coats the meat and begins to caramelize slightly. Season with white pepper.
5
Add Basil
Remove from heat. Add Thai basil leaves and toss — the residual heat wilts the basil perfectly. Don't cook basil over direct heat or it loses its fragrance.
6
Serve
Plate over cauliflower rice with a crispy fried egg on top. Serve with lime wedges and extra chili flakes.

Notes

Thai basil has an anise-clove note that's distinctly different from Italian basil — worth seeking out at an Asian grocery.
The crispy-edged fried egg is not optional — it's an essential component of pad kra pao.
High heat is non-negotiable for this dish. A screaming-hot wok gives you the 'wok hei' (breath of the wok) that makes Thai stir-fry taste like restaurant food.
This is the ground turkey recipe that converts skeptics — the flavor is assertive enough to completely transform the protein.
