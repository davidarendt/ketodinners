Ground Beef Stuffed Poblano Peppers

⏱ 30 min🍽 4 servings✓ Strict Keto
440Calories
34gProtein
28gFat
9gNet Carbs

Charred poblano peppers stuffed with a smoky ground beef and cheese filling — cumin, chili powder, garlic, and black beans (swapped for more beef to keep it keto) — broiled until the cheese bubbles and the pepper blisters. More interesting than bell peppers, with a gentle, fruity heat.

Ingredients
Peppers & Filling
·4 large poblano peppers
·1.25 lbs ground beef (80/20)
·1/2 yellow onion, diced
·3 cloves garlic, minced
·1 tsp cumin
·1 tsp chili powder
·1/2 tsp smoked paprika
·1/2 tsp oregano
·1 can (10 oz) diced tomatoes with green chiles (Rotel), drained
·Salt and black pepper
Topping & Finish
·1.5 cups shredded Mexican blend cheese (Monterey Jack + cheddar)
·Sour cream to serve
·Sliced avocado
·Fresh cilantro
·Lime wedges
Instructions
1
Char the Poblanos
Place poblanos directly over a gas burner flame or under a broiler. Turn with tongs every 2–3 minutes until completely charred and blistered on all sides — about 8–10 minutes. Place in a sealed zip-lock bag for 5 minutes to steam, then peel off the charred skin. Cut a slit lengthwise and remove seeds carefully.
2
Brown the Beef
While poblanos char, cook ground beef in a skillet over high heat, breaking up, until well browned. Drain excess fat.
3
Build the Filling
Add onion and cook 3 minutes. Add garlic, cumin, chili powder, paprika, and oregano. Stir 30 seconds. Add drained Rotel. Season with salt and pepper. Cook 2–3 minutes until mostly dry.
4
Stuff & Top
Arrange peeled poblanos in a baking dish. Stuff each generously with the beef filling. Top with shredded cheese.
5
Broil
Broil 3–4 minutes until cheese is melted, bubbly, and starting to brown.
6
Serve
Top with sour cream, sliced avocado, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime.

Notes

Charring the poblanos is the most important step — it removes bitterness and develops a subtle smoky sweetness.
Poblanos are milder than jalapeños but have far more flavor than bell peppers — they're the upgrade your stuffed pepper game needs.
The steam-and-peel step is easy; the charred skin comes off in large pieces after 5 minutes in the bag.
Rotel (diced tomatoes with green chiles) adds depth and moisture without adding significant carbs.
